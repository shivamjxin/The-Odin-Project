Crêpes

Description

Crêpes are a beloved French delicacy known for their thin, delicate texture and versatility. Made from a simple batter of flour, eggs, milk, and a pinch of salt, these paper-thin pancakes are cooked on a hot griddle until lightly golden. Their neutral flavor makes them the perfect canvas for both sweet and savory fillings, ranging from fresh fruits, Nutella, and whipped cream to ham, cheese, and sautéed vegetables. The light, airy nature of crêpes allows the flavors of the fillings to shine through, creating a delightful balance in every bite.

What truly sets crêpes apart is their ability to cater to a wide array of tastes and occasions. Whether enjoyed as a decadent dessert, a light breakfast, or a savory main course, crêpes offer endless possibilities for customization. Their elegant appearance and delicate taste make them a popular choice for brunches, special gatherings, and even casual meals. With their rich culinary heritage and global appeal, crêpes continue to enchant food lovers with their charming simplicity and sophisticated flavors.

Ingredients

You need just five ingredients (plus some warm water) to make this super simple pizza crust.
  • Milk
    1 ½ cups milk
  • Egg Yolk
    3 egg yolks.
  • Vanilla Extract
    2 tablespoons vanilla extract.
  • All-Purpose flour
    1 ½ cups all-purpose flour.
  • Salt
    ½ teaspoon salt.
  • Butter
    5 tablespoons melted butter
  • Sugar
    2 tablespoons sugar

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the milk, egg yolks and vanilla. Stir in the flour, sugar, salt and melted butter until well blended.
  2. Heat a crepe pan over medium heat until hot. Coat with vegetable oil or cooking spray. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter into the pan and tip to spread the batter to the edges. When bubbles form on the top and the edges are dry, flip over and cook until lightly browned on the other side and edges are golden. Repeat with remaining batter.
  3. Fill crepes with your favorite fruit, cream, caramel or even ice cream or cheese to serve.
